How big is space

Today we are fairly confident that the Milky Way is probably between 100,000 and 150,000 light years across. The observable Universe is, of course, much larger. According to current thinking it is about 93 billion light years in diameter.

This distance was measured using a way we use everyday, they used the same technice as we humans use to detemine distance using our two eyes. The set up two telescopes and measured the angles to the star, then they did some math and calculated the distance.
